About The Festival

Auckland's Burmese
New Year Celebration

သင်္ကြန်ပွဲတော် — မြန်မာ့နှစ်သစ်ကူး ကြိုဆိုပါ

Thingyan (သင်္ကြန်) is Myanmar's most beloved festival — a vibrant, joyful celebration marking the transition into the Burmese New Year. Observed every year in mid-April, it is a time of cleansing, renewal, and community spirit, deeply rooted in over 900 years of Buddhist tradition.

At the heart of Thingyan is the symbolic act of pouring water — washing away the troubles and misfortunes of the year gone by, and welcoming fresh beginnings with an open heart. Here in Auckland, we carry that tradition with pride.

💧
Water Splashing — Symbolises the cleansing of sins and the welcoming of good fortune into the New Year.
🌸
Padauk Flowers — The golden padauk (ပိတောက်) blooms during Thingyan season, worn as a symbol of joy and the festival spirit.
🙏
Merit-Making — A time to show respect to elders, make offerings, and practise generosity within the community.

Burmese Food Stalls

Taste of
Myanmar

Authentic home-cooked Burmese dishes prepared by our community. From savoury favourites to sweet Thingyan treats, there's something for everyone.

🍜
Mohinga
မုန့်ဟင်းခါး
Myanmar's beloved national dish — rice noodles in a rich, aromatic fish and lemongrass broth. The soul of Burmese street food.
🥗
Laphet Thoke
လက်ဖက်သုပ်
Famous fermented tea leaf salad with crunchy nuts, sesame seeds, and a tangy, umami-rich dressing. A true Myanmar classic.
🍛
Burmese Curry
ဟင်းသီးဟင်းရွက်ဟင်း
Slow-cooked Burmese curry — chicken, beef, or vegetable — rich with turmeric, ginger, and aromatic spices. Served with steamed rice.
🍜
Ono Khauk Swe
အုန်းနို့ခေါက်ဆွဲ
Creamy coconut milk noodle soup topped with crispy noodles, egg, and a squeeze of lime. Warming, rich, and deeply satisfying.
🍡
Mont Lone Yay Baw
မုန့်လုံးရေပေါ်
The iconic Thingyan sweet! Glutinous rice balls filled with jaggery sugar, coated in fresh coconut. A festival must-eat.
🍨
Shwe Yin Aye
ရွှေရည်အေး
A refreshing Thingyan dessert — coconut milk, sago, jelly, and sweet toppings served chilled. Perfect for a warm Auckland evening.
🍮
Mont Pyit Thalet
မုန့်ပျဉ်သလက်
Sticky rice pancakes with coconut and palm sugar filling — a beloved Burmese snack that disappears from the stalls quickly!
🥥
Fresh Coconut Water
အုန်းရည်
Ice-cold fresh coconut water — the perfect refreshment after the water-splashing festivities outside.

Practical Info

What to Expect

A few things to know before you come, so you can make the most of the evening.

01
Wear Traditional or Casual Dress
We love seeing traditional Myanmar longyi and htamein at Thingyan! Casual western dress is perfectly welcome too. Whatever makes you feel festive.
02
Bring a Change of Clothes
The outdoor water-splashing area is all in good fun — but you may want a dry set of clothes for the rest of the evening's performances!
03
Cash & Eftpos for Food Stalls
Food is not included in the ticket price. Food stalls accept cash and eftpos. Prices are very affordable — budgeting $10–15 per person covers a hearty meal and desserts.
04
All Nationalities Welcome
This is a community celebration for everyone! Bring your Kiwi friends, neighbours, and colleagues — Thingyan is one of the world's most joyful festivals and we love sharing it.
05
Respect the Tradition
Please be mindful not to splash water on monks, elderly guests, or pregnant women during the water activity. Water-splashing is a joyful act — please keep it kind and gentle.
06
Photography Welcome
Photos and videos are welcome throughout the event. The performances are made to be shared — please tag us and use our event hashtag to share the joy!
